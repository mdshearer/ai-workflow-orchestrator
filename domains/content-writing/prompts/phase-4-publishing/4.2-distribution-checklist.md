# Prompt: Content Strategist - Distribution & Promotion Plan

**Persona:** Content Strategist
**Phase:** 4 - Publishing
**Purpose:** Create distribution and promotion plan to maximize content reach and impact

---

## When to Use This Prompt

✅ **Use when:** Content is published or ready to publish, need promotion strategy
❌ **Don't use when:** Content isn't finalized or you have no distribution channels

---

## The Prompt Template

```markdown
You are a Content Strategist. Your task is to create a distribution and promotion plan to maximize this content's reach.

**Rules:**
1. The distribution plan must be in Markdown format.
2. The output must be saved as `artifacts/07-distribution-checklist.md`
3. The plan must include:
   - Owned channels (email, social media, website)
   - Paid promotion opportunities (if budget available)
   - Earned media opportunities (outreach, partnerships)
   - Internal linking strategy
   - Performance tracking plan
   - Timeline (launch day, week 1, week 2, ongoing)
4. Prioritize channels where target audience is most active

**Context:**
- Target audience: [AUDIENCE]
- Content goal: [GOAL] (traffic, leads, brand awareness)
- Available channels: [CHANNELS] (email list, social media, etc.)
- Budget: [BUDGET] (if applicable for paid promotion)

**Files to Read:**
- `artifacts/02-draft-v4-FINAL.md` (for content summary)
- `artifacts/01-content-brief.md` (for goals and audience)
- `CONSTITUTION.md` (for distribution standards)

---

Please create a distribution plan for this content:

[Topic or reference to final draft]

```

---

## Quick Start

Content Strategist will create plan for:
1. Owned channels (email, social, blog)
2. Paid promotion (if budget available)
3. Partnerships and outreach
4. Internal site promotion
5. Performance tracking

Execute plan to maximize content ROI.

---

## Example Output

```markdown
# Distribution & Promotion Checklist

## Content Summary
**Title:** How to Choose Project Management Software for Small Teams
**URL:** /how-to-choose-project-management-software-small-teams
**Target:** 2,000 monthly visits, 50 trial signups
**Audience:** Product managers, team leads at tech startups (10-20 people)

## Launch Day (Day 0)

### Owned Channels
- [ ] Publish to blog (morning, 9am EST)
- [ ] Email newsletter to subscribers (15,000 people)
  - Subject: "Overwhelmed by PM tool options? This guide helps"
  - Segment: Startups + small teams
- [ ] Post to company LinkedIn (link to article)
- [ ] Post to company Twitter/X (thread format, 3-4 tweets)
- [ ] Share in company Slack #content channel

### Internal Site
- [ ] Add to homepage "Latest Posts" section
- [ ] Link from related articles:
  - "Best PM Tools for Startups" (add in intro)
  - "Managing Remote Teams" (add in resources section)
- [ ] Feature in sidebar "Recommended Reading"

## Week 1 (Days 1-7)

### Social Media Schedule
- **Day 2:** LinkedIn (different angle: cost comparison)
- **Day 4:** Twitter/X (quote from article + link)
- **Day 6:** LinkedIn (infographic: 5 criteria)

### Community Outreach
- [ ] Share in relevant Reddit communities (r/startups, r/projectmanagement)
  - Note: Add value, don't spam - answer questions and share if relevant
- [ ] Share in IndieHackers community
- [ ] Post in ProductHunt "Ask PH" if relevant

### Email Drip
- [ ] Add to onboarding email sequence (for new trial users)
- [ ] Include in weekly digest email (Day 7)

## Week 2 (Days 8-14)

### Paid Promotion (If Budget Available)
- [ ] LinkedIn sponsored post ($200 budget)
  - Target: Product Managers, CTOs at startups (10-100 employees)
  - Goal: 5,000 impressions, 100 clicks
- [ ] Reddit promoted post in r/startups ($100 budget)
  - Goal: 2,000 impressions, 50 clicks

### Partnerships & Outreach
- [ ] Reach out to 5 relevant newsletters for feature/link
  - Identify: PM tool review sites, startup newsletters
  - Pitch: "Comprehensive guide your readers would find valuable"
- [ ] Contact 3 complementary SaaS companies for guest post swap
- [ ] Submit to content aggregators (if applicable)

## Ongoing (Weeks 3+)

### Evergreen Promotion
- [ ] Add to standard email signature (link in PS)
- [ ] Include in sales team resources (for prospect education)
- [ ] Reference in customer onboarding materials
- [ ] Repurpose into other formats:
  - 5-minute video summary (YouTube, LinkedIn)
  - Slide deck (SlideShare)
  - Infographic (Pinterest, Instagram)
  - Podcast episode (if you have a podcast)

### SEO & Updates
- [ ] Monitor rankings (track primary keyword weekly)
- [ ] Update with new tools/pricing (quarterly review)
- [ ] Add to "Last updated: [date]" to maintain freshness

## Performance Tracking

### Metrics to Track
- **Traffic:**
  - Page views (goal: 2,000/month by month 6)
  - Unique visitors
  - Traffic sources (organic, social, email, etc.)
- **Engagement:**
  - Avg time on page (goal: 3+ minutes)
  - Bounce rate (goal: <40%)
  - Scroll depth (% reaching conclusion)
- **Conversions:**
  - CTA clicks (trial signups)
  - Goal: 50 signups/month by month 6
- **SEO:**
  - Keyword rankings (primary + secondary)
  - Backlinks acquired
  - Domain authority impact

### Tracking Tools
- Google Analytics (traffic, engagement)
- Google Search Console (rankings, impressions)
- Ahrefs/SEMrush (backlinks, keyword tracking)
- CMS analytics (if applicable)

### Review Schedule
- **Week 1:** Initial performance check (traffic sources, engagement)
- **Month 1:** Full analysis (traffic, rankings, conversions)
- **Month 3:** Optimization review (update content if needed)
- **Month 6:** ROI assessment (goal achievement, next steps)

## Success Criteria

### Month 1 Goals
- 500 page views
- 10 trial signups
- Ranking in top 50 for primary keyword

### Month 3 Goals
- 1,000 page views/month
- 25 trial signups/month
- Ranking in top 20 for primary keyword

### Month 6 Goals (Target)
- 2,000 page views/month ✅
- 50 trial signups/month ✅
- Ranking in top 10 for primary keyword ✅

## Notes
- Repurpose high-performing sections into standalone social posts
- Monitor comments/questions and engage (builds community)
- Track which distribution channels drive most conversions (double down on winners)
```
