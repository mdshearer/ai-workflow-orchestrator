# Prompt: SEO Specialist - Keyword Research

**Persona:** SEO Specialist
**Phase:** 1 - Planning
**Purpose:** Research and identify primary and secondary keywords with search volume, competition, and search intent analysis

---

## When to Use This Prompt

✅ **Use when:** You need deeper keyword analysis beyond the content brief, or starting keyword research from scratch
❌ **Don't use when:** Keywords are already defined in the content brief and don't need further research

---

## The Prompt Template

```markdown
You are an SEO Specialist. Your task is to research keywords for our content and provide strategic recommendations.

**Rules:**
1. You must adhere to all principles in the attached `CONSTITUTION.md` file.
2. The keyword research report must be in Markdown format.
3. The output must be saved as `artifacts/01-keyword-strategy.md`
4. The report must include:
   - Primary keyword recommendation (1 keyword)
   - Secondary keywords (5-10 related terms)
   - Search volume estimates (if available)
   - Competition assessment (low, medium, high)
   - Search intent for each keyword (informational, commercial, navigational, transactional)
   - Long-tail keyword variations
   - Question-based keywords ("how to...", "what is...")
   - Competitive keyword gap analysis
5. **CRITICAL:** Recommend realistic keywords we can rank for, not just high-volume impossible targets.

**Output Format:**
- Save as `artifacts/01-keyword-strategy.md`
- Include all required sections
- Use tables for keyword comparisons

**Context:**
- Content topic: [CONTENT_TOPIC]
- Target audience: [TARGET_AUDIENCE]
- Domain authority (if known): [DOMAIN_AUTHORITY]
- Current ranking keywords (if any): [EXISTING_KEYWORDS]

**Files to Read:**
- `CONSTITUTION.md` (for SEO standards and targets)
- `artifacts/01-content-brief.md` (if it exists - for context)

---

Here is the content topic for keyword research:

[YOUR_TOPIC_DESCRIPTION]

---

**Additional Context** (optional but helpful):
- Competitor URLs to analyze: [COMPETITOR_URLS]
- Keywords we're currently ranking for: [EXISTING_KEYWORDS]
- Geographic target: [LOCATION] (e.g., US, UK, global)
```

---

## Quick Start Guide

### Step 1: Prepare
- [ ] Know your content topic
- [ ] Understand your target audience
- [ ] (Optional) Know your domain authority
- [ ] (Optional) Have competitor URLs to analyze

### Step 2: Fill Template Variables
- Replace `[CONTENT_TOPIC]` → e.g., "Choosing project management software"
- Replace `[TARGET_AUDIENCE]` → e.g., "Small tech team leaders"
- Replace `[DOMAIN_AUTHORITY]` → e.g., "DA 35" or "Unknown"
- Replace `[YOUR_TOPIC_DESCRIPTION]` → Describe the topic

### Step 3: Invoke AI
The SEO Specialist will analyze:
- Search volume for potential keywords
- Competition level (can we rank?)
- Search intent (what do searchers want?)
- Related keywords and variations

### Step 4: Review Keyword Strategy
Check:
- [ ] Primary keyword is realistic (not impossible to rank)
- [ ] Search intent matches content type
- [ ] Secondary keywords support topic comprehensively
- [ ] Long-tail variations are included

### Step 5: Integrate into Content Brief
Add keywords to `artifacts/01-content-brief.md` or use in next phase

---

## Tips for Better Keyword Research

✅ **DO:**
- Target realistic keywords (match your domain authority)
- Prioritize search intent over search volume
- Include question-based keywords (great for content structure)
- Look for keyword gaps competitors miss
- Consider long-tail variations (easier to rank)

❌ **DON'T:**
- Target ultra-competitive keywords you can't rank for
- Ignore search intent (informational vs commercial)
- Stuff too many keywords into one piece
- Only look at search volume (competition matters)
- Forget about user intent

## Example Output Structure

```markdown
# Keyword Strategy: Project Management Software Selection

## Primary Keyword Recommendation

**Keyword:** "how to choose project management software for small teams"
- **Search Volume:** ~1,200/month
- **Competition:** Medium (achievable)
- **Search Intent:** Informational (researching before purchase)
- **Why this keyword:** Long-tail, specific audience, matches our content angle

## Secondary Keywords (8)

| Keyword | Volume | Competition | Intent | Priority |
|---------|--------|-------------|--------|----------|
| project management tools for startups | 800/mo | Medium | Informational | High |
| simple PM software | 400/mo | Low | Commercial | High |
| PM tool comparison | 600/mo | High | Commercial | Medium |
| best task management for small teams | 500/mo | Medium | Commercial | High |
| team collaboration software | 2,000/mo | High | Commercial | Low |

## Long-Tail Variations (6)

- "how to select PM software for tech startups"
- "choosing PM tools for remote teams"
- "simple project management for small businesses"
- "PM software under $15 per user"
- "best PM tools for Agile teams under 20 people"

## Question-Based Keywords (5)

- "How do you choose project management software?"
- "What project management tool is best for small teams?"
- "Which PM software is easiest to use?"
- "How much should PM software cost per user?"

## Search Intent Analysis

### Informational Intent (Use for guides, how-tos)
- "how to choose project management software"
- "what is project management software"
- "project management best practices"

### Commercial Intent (Use for comparisons, reviews)
- "best project management tools"
- "PM software comparison"
- "project management tool reviews"

### Transactional Intent (Use for landing pages)
- "buy project management software"
- "PM software free trial"
- "sign up for PM tool"

## Competitive Analysis

**Top Ranking Content:**
1. CompetitorA.com/pm-guide (DA 65) - Comprehensive 3,000-word guide
2. CompetitorB.com/pm-tools (DA 58) - Comparison with pricing table
3. CompetitorC.com/choosing-pm (DA 42) - How-to guide with tool examples

**Keyword Gaps (Opportunities):**
- "PM software for distributed teams" - low competition, no strong content
- "simple PM tools without complexity" - underserved niche
- "PM software onboarding best practices" - related topic, low competition

## Recommendations

1. **Primary keyword:** Use "how to choose project management software for small teams" in title, H1, first 100 words
2. **Secondary keywords:** Incorporate naturally in H2 subheadings
3. **Content structure:** Build sections around question-based keywords (great for featured snippets)
4. **Target length:** 1,800-2,200 words (matches top-ranking content)
5. **Differentiator:** Focus on "simplicity" angle (keyword gap: "simple PM software")

## Next Steps

1. Integrate primary keyword into content brief
2. Use secondary keywords to refine H2/H3 outline
3. Incorporate question-based keywords as subheadings
4. Proceed to draft creation
```
