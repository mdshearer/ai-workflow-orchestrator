# Prompt: Content Strategist - Content Outline Creation

**Persona:** Content Strategist
**Phase:** 1 - Planning
**Purpose:** Create detailed H2/H3 content outline that serves audience needs and SEO strategy

---

## When to Use This Prompt

✅ **Use when:** You have keywords and topic defined, need to build logical content structure
❌ **Don't use when:** Outline already exists in content brief or you're ready to draft

---

## The Prompt Template

```markdown
You are a Content Strategist. Your task is to create a detailed content outline with H2/H3 structure.

**Rules:**
1. You must adhere to all principles in the attached `CONSTITUTION.md` file.
2. The outline must be in Markdown format.
3. The output must be saved as `artifacts/01-content-outline.md`
4. The outline must include:
   - Article title (H1) with primary keyword
   - Introduction structure (hook, problem, solution preview)
   - 3-5 main H2 sections
   - 2-4 H3 subsections under each H2
   - Conclusion structure (summary, CTA)
   - Estimated word counts per section
5. **CRITICAL:** Structure should serve the reader's journey (problem → understanding → solution).

**Context:**
- Primary keyword: [PRIMARY_KEYWORD]
- Target word count: [WORD_COUNT] words
- Target audience: [TARGET_AUDIENCE]
- Content angle: [UNIQUE_ANGLE]

**Files to Read:**
- `CONSTITUTION.md` (for content standards)
- `artifacts/01-content-brief.md` (for topic and keywords)
- `artifacts/01-keyword-strategy.md` (if exists - for secondary keywords)

---

Here is the content topic:

[YOUR_TOPIC]

---

**Additional Context:**
- Question-based keywords to include: [QUESTIONS]
- Sections to cover: [REQUIRED_SECTIONS]
```

---

## Quick Start Guide

### Step 1: Fill Variables
- Replace `[PRIMARY_KEYWORD]` → Main keyword
- Replace `[WORD_COUNT]` → Target length (e.g., 1,800)
- Replace `[TARGET_AUDIENCE]` → Who you're writing for
- Replace `[UNIQUE_ANGLE]` → Your differentiation

### Step 2: Invoke AI
The Strategist will create a logical outline serving the audience's journey

### Step 3: Review Outline
Check:
- [ ] Logical flow (intro → body → conclusion)
- [ ] Each H2 covers distinct sub-topic
- [ ] Secondary keywords in H2s
- [ ] Question-based keywords as H3s

---

## Example Output

```markdown
# How to Choose Project Management Software for Small Teams

## Introduction (150 words)
- Hook: "73% of small teams struggle with PM tool complexity"
- Problem: Most PM software built for enterprises
- Solution preview: 5 criteria framework

## H2: Why Most PM Tools Fail Small Teams (300 words)
### H3: Feature overload kills adoption
### H3: Enterprise pricing doesn't fit small budgets
### H3: Complexity slows down productivity

## H2: The 5 Criteria for Choosing PM Software (800 words)
### H3: Usability (learn in <1 hour)
### H3: Fair pricing ($5-15/user/month)
### H3: Essential integrations only
### H3: Responsive support
### H3: Room to grow

## H2: Top PM Tools by Use Case (500 words)
### H3: Best for tech startups
### H3: Best for remote teams
### H3: Best for Agile teams

## Conclusion & CTA (150 words)
- Summary: 5 criteria decision framework
- CTA: Start free trial
```
